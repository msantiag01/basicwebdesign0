History About The Park

Death Valley National Park has a rich and unique history that dates back thousands of years. It's home to the Timbasha Shoshone Native American tribe and Mojave Desert. The national park is located in Nevada and Eastern regions of California. The park spans across approxamintly 3 million acres of designated protected land. Explorers traveled to this region in search of gold and other resources during the Gold Rush in the 1800's.

Death Valley was established as a national park in 1994 thanks to President Hoover. Today, visitors can explore the park's history through its many historical sites, including abandoned mining towns, ancient petroglyphs, and remnants of the area's pioneer past.

Planning A Visit?

Death Valley National Park is a fascinationg and unique destination that is worth visiting. According to the National Park Service over 1 million people visited the park in 2021. If you are planning a visit, having essential items like water and sunscreen is a must have.
