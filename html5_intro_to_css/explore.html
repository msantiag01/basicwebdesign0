Things To Do

There are many things to do and see in Death Valley National Park. The park offers many activities for visitors of all ages and interests. Outdoor enthusiasts can explore the park's stunning landscapes on foot, with hiking trails ranging from easy walks to the Zxabriski Point or strenuous climbs to the Mosaic Canyon. The park also offers opportunities for camping amd stargazing. Listed below are popular spots you must see on your trip through Death Valley.

  • Zabriski Point
  • Natural Bridge Canyon
  • Bad Water Basin
  • Mesquite Flat Sand Dunes

Life In Death Valley

One common misconception about Death Valley is that it's uninhabitable and just a wasteland. Despite its harsh conditions, the park is home to a diverse array of plant and animal species that have evolved unique adaptations to survive in this extreme environment. Visitors can spot desert bighorn sheep, desert tortoise, and other wildlife, as well as explore the park's many unique plant communities, such as Joshua trees and desert wildflowers. Overall, life in Death Valley National Park is a fascinating and ever-evolving ecosystem that inspires awe and respect for the natural world.
